Greetings! I am a PhD candidate in [Linguistics at the University of Florida](https://lin.ufl.edu/), working with Dr. Ratree Wayland. I study speech production and perception, with a particular interest in how linguistic representations are translated into coordinated speech movements. My work uses articulatory and acoustic methods, including electromagnetic articulography (EMA), to examine speech motor learning, coordination, and variability in both native and second-language speech.

I received my B.A. in Linguistics (*summa cum laude*) from the University of Florida in 2019. My research is grounded in experimental, data-driven approaches to articulatory and acoustic phonetics, and my work has appeared in journals such as *The Journal of the Acoustical Society of America*, *Journal of Speech, Language, and Hearing Research*, *Applied Psycholinguistics*, and *Brain Sciences*. Outside of academia, I enjoy hiking and reading Russian poetry.
